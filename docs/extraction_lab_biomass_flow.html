Extraction Lab Biomass Flow

Step-by-step process from charging a biomass lot into extraction through the first wet THCA and HTE pour / output confirmation.

Built From Implemented extraction app workflow, run progression state machine, run form fields, and booth SOP alignment notes in this repo.
Audience Extraction operators, shift leads, supervisors, and production trainers.
Format Landscape workflow table with process name, responsible role, and time / timing expectation.
Boundary Ends when wet THCA and wet HTE are poured / weighed and initial outputs are confirmed. Later drying, milling, terp stripping, and destination queues are outside this document.
This is an operational training map inferred from the current software and planning documents. It is not a replacement for the approved safety SOP, equipment manuals, or state compliance requirements.

How To Read This

The rows are ordered in the sequence the team should experience the run. Branches are labeled where the workflow can split or loop.

App checkpoint: the current app has a button, field, timer, or stored timestamp for this.
Manual / inferred: the software implies this operational work but may not capture it as its own step.
Critical control: prerequisite, measurement, evidence, or safety-sensitive decision.
Branch / loop: the process changes direction based on an operator decision.
Handoff: the extraction run closes and wet outputs are transferred to the downstream process.
Suggested roles use the app's apparent launch-role model: extractor/operator, supervisor/shift lead, downstream operator, and maintenance/facilities where relevant.

Phase 1 - Charge, Reactor Prep, And Primary Extraction

Biomass lot enters extraction and primary solvent contact is completed
Step Name Of Process Person / Role Responsible Time What Happens / Critical Notes
Lot selection and charge creation
1
Scan or enter biomass lot
App		 Extractor / operator Start of extraction session Operator scans or types the tracking ID in the standalone extraction app. The app opens the lot charge workflow and shows source, supplier, strain, and available biomass context.
2
Confirm lot is ready to charge
Critical		 Extractor; supervisor if exception Before biomass is loaded Confirm the lot is available, correct, and ready. The app has ready/not-ready state and charge warnings. Do not proceed if the selected lot, weight, or reactor is wrong.
3
Record extraction charge
App		 Extractor / operator When biomass is committed to a reactor Choose reactor and charge weight. The current app favors 100 lb, Half lot, Full lot, or Last used presets. Recording the charge links the lot, reactor, and draft run.
4
Physically load biomass / baskets into reactor
Inferred		 Extractor / operator Immediately after charge is recorded Load the charged biomass into the selected reactor using site SOP. App stores charge weight, fill count, total fill weight, stringer basket count, and blend %, but does not currently spell out every physical loading movement.
5
Confirm reactor/tank readiness
Inferred		 Extractor; maintenance if needed Before confirming under vacuum Confirm reactor, recovery path, filtration path, and relevant tanks are clean, assembled, closed/open as required, and safe to pull down. This is critical but not fully modeled as a separate app checkpoint.
Primary extraction
6
Confirm under vacuum
Critical		 Extractor / operator Before solvent charge App button: Confirm Under Vacuum. This records timestamp/operator and is required before solvent charging can continue.
7
Prepare solvent supply / tank charge path
Inferred		 Extractor; maintenance/facilities as assigned Before or during solvent charge setup Confirm solvent source/tank is ready and valve path is correct. The app captures solvent charge amount, not the full equipment prep sequence.
8
Record primary solvent charge
Critical		 Extractor / operator After vacuum; before soak App field/button: Primary Solvent Charge, typically 500 lb per placeholder/default language. Weight must be positive. Timestamp is stored when Record Solvent Charge is pressed.
9
Start primary soak
App		 Extractor / operator Target: 30 min App button: Start Primary Soak. Timing target is settings-driven and currently defaults to 30 minutes. Timing misses can warn, require override, or hard stop depending on settings.
10
Start mixer / agitation
App		 Extractor / operator During primary soak; target: 5 min App button: Start Mixer. The mixer stage is timed separately so the operator can document agitation during the soak.
11
Stop mixer
App		 Extractor / operator After agitation target App button: Stop Mixer. This completes mixer timing and advances the operator to the filter-clear checkpoint.
12
Confirm basket filter clear
Critical		 Extractor / operator After mixer; before pressurization App button: Confirm Filter Clear. The SOP plan describes this as clearing the basket filter before nitrogen pressurization.
13
Start nitrogen pressurization
Critical		 Extractor / operator After filter clear App button: Start Pressurization. This begins the pressure step that moves the process toward recovery.
14
Begin recovery / flow to filtration and recovery
App		 Extractor / operator After pressurization begins App button: Begin Recovery. This checkpoint represents sending gas/liquid through filtration and recovery. Heat-exchanger handling is implied by the SOP plan but not currently its own app button.

Phase 2 - Flush Cycle, Recovery, And Final Purge

Transition from primary extraction into flush
15
Begin flush cycle
App		 Extractor / operator After primary recovery checkpoint App button: Begin Flush Cycle. This moves the run from primary extraction checkpoints into flush controls.
16
Verify flush solvent and plate temperatures
Critical		 Extractor / operator; supervisor if out of range Before flush solvent charge App fields: Flush Solvent Chiller Temp and Plate Temp. Placeholder indicates -40 F or below for solvent chiller. Current app also supports evidence uploads for solvent chiller and plate temp photos.

Post / document temperature evidence
Partially captured		 Extractor / operator With temperature verification The app has a Posted to Slack checkbox and photo evidence counts. Slack reliability was previously deferred, so the app should be treated as the stronger system record for temp proof.
18
Record flush solvent charge
Critical		 Extractor / operator After temp verification App field/button: Flush Solvent Charge, typically 500 lb per placeholder/default language. Requires temp verification in the guided flow.
19
Start flush soak
App		 Extractor / operator Target: 10 min App button: Start Flush. Timing target is settings-driven and defaults to 10 minutes. The run also stores flush count and total flush weight.
20
Start flush mixer
App		 Extractor / operator Last 5 min of flush soak App button: Start Flush Mixer. Default window opens at minute 5 of a 10-minute flush soak. A reason field appears when starting outside the configured window.
21
End flush mixer
App		 Extractor / operator During flush soak App button: End Flush Mixer. Default target runtime is 5 minutes. End flush mixer before Stop Flush when the mixer was started.
22
Burp / re-pressurization valve handling
Inferred		 Extractor / operator During or after flush soak Physical burp and re-pressurization steps from the SOP are not yet separate app checkpoints; surrounding flush timing and flow-resumed decision capture the handoff.
23
Stop flush
App		 Extractor / operator After flush target / when flush is complete App button: Stop Flush. This closes the flush soak timer and advances the process to the flow-resumed decision. Blocked while flush mixer is still running.
Branch A/B - flow resumed decision
22A
Branch A - flow resumed
Branch A		 Extractor / operator After flush stop App button/decision: Confirm Flow Resumed. If yes, the run advances to final purge.
22B
Branch B - flow not resumed; adjust and re-check
Branch B		 Extractor / operator; supervisor if repeated issue Until flow resumes If flow has not resumed, the app enters Flow Adjustment Required and offers Re-check Flow. Repeat adjustments and decision until the flow decision is yes.
Final purge, clarity, and booth shutdown
23
Start final purge
App		 Extractor / operator After flow resumed is confirmed App button: Start Final Purge. Optional target duration can be configured in Settings; default policy is informational unless tightened.
24
Stop final purge
App		 Extractor / operator When purge/burp is complete App button: Stop Final Purge. Duration is computed from start/end timestamps.
Branch C/D - final clarity decision
25C
Branch C - final clarity confirmed
Branch C		 Extractor / operator After final purge App button/decision: Confirm Final Clarity. If yes, the process can move to shutdown checklist.
25D
Branch D - more purge / clarity work required
Branch D		 Extractor / operator; supervisor if repeated issue Until clear enough to proceed If not clear enough, the app enters More Purge / Clarity Work Required and offers Resume Final Purge. Repeat final purge and clarity confirmation until clear.
26
Complete shutdown checklist
Critical		 Extractor / operator After final clarity is confirmed App checklist fields: Recovery inlets closed, Filtration pump-down started, Nitrogen off, Dewax inlet closed. These are captured as shutdown timestamps before closing the booth process.
27
Mark run complete
App		 Extractor / operator After shutdown checklist App button: Mark Run Complete. This records run completion and completes the linked reactor event when it is still active.

Phase 3 - Wet THCA / HTE Pour And Handoff

Select post-extraction pathway
Handoff		 Extractor or supervisor After run completion; before downstream starts App choices: 100 lb pot pour or 200 lb minor run. The current question stops at the initial THCA/HTE pour, but the pathway should be selected so downstream knows what workflow owns the material next.
29
Start post-extraction handoff
Handoff		 Extractor / downstream operator Immediately before pour/output capture App button: Start Post-Extraction. This is blocked until the extraction run is complete.
30
Prepare receiving containers and labels
Inferred		 Extractor / downstream operator Before opening/pouring outputs Prepare labeled containers for wet THCA and wet HTE. The app captures weights but does not currently enforce container labeling, tare, or label scan steps in this exact handoff.
31
Pour / collect wet HTE
Critical		 Extractor / downstream operator During output pour Collect wet HTE into the correct receiving container. Record Wet HTE in grams in the app before confirming initial outputs.
32
Pour / collect wet THCA
Critical		 Extractor / downstream operator During output pour Collect wet THCA into the correct receiving container. Record Wet THCA in grams in the app before confirming initial outputs.
33
Confirm initial outputs
App		 Extractor or supervisor After both wet output weights are entered App button: Confirm Initial Outputs. This requires both Wet HTE and Wet THCA. It timestamps the handoff and allows the chosen downstream branch to continue.
34
Clean / reset extraction equipment for next run
Inferred		 Extractor; maintenance/facilities as assigned After output pour and shutdown, before next charge Clean tanks, lines, reactor, filtration path, and receiving area according to SOP. The current app does not make cleaning a first-class completion gate, but production feedback suggests it should be visible in the operator workflow.

Suggested Additions To Make This Easier To Follow

  • Add a one-page laminated “Run Card” with the same step numbers as the app buttons.
  • Group the tablet workflow into four big phases: Charge, Primary, Flush/Purge, Pour/Handoff.
  • Add a visual progress rail with Branch A/B and Branch C/D loops shown directly on screen.
  • Make prep and cleaning visible: reactor ready, solvent tank ready, containers labeled, post-run clean/reset.
  • Use role initials on each step: EXT, SUP, DOWN, MAINT.

Process Gaps Worth Confirming With The Team

  • Exact owner and timing for tank cleaning, solvent loading, and reactor readiness checks.
  • Whether -40 F is a hard stop or a supervisor-overridable warning in production.
  • Whether Slack evidence remains required or app photo evidence replaces it as the source of truth.
  • Whether wet HTE and wet THCA should require container labels, tare weights, photos, or supervisor signoff.
  • Whether run complete should mean booth shutdown complete or output pour complete.
