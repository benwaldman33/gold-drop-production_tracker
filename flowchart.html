Key Notes

  • Single Purchase entity: A Purchase record tracks the full lifecycle from supplier declaration through delivery. Status flow: Declared → In Testing (optional) → Committed → Delivered. Old uncommitted records age out automatically based on configurable day thresholds.
  • Entry points: Purchases can be created from the biomass pipeline form, field intake (mobile), Slack import, spreadsheet import, or direct entry. All entry points create Purchase records.
  • Pipeline auto-approval: When a Purchase moves to Committed via the biomass pipeline, it is automatically approved (since commitment requires approver permissions). Field submission approvals also auto-approve.
  • Purchase Approval Gate: Purchases created via Slack import, spreadsheet import, or manual entry are not approved by default. An authorized user must explicitly approve before lots become visible in inventory or available for extraction runs.
  • Inventory split: In-Transit = committed/ordered/in-transit purchases. On-Hand = delivered/available/processing purchases that are approved. Days of Supply = approved on-hand lbs ÷ daily throughput target.
  • Extraction runs: Consume inventory lots from approved purchases only (decrements remaining weight) and produce THCA + HTE outputs. Yields and cost-per-gram auto-calculate.
  • HTE post-processing: Runs with HTE output move through a lab pipeline: Awaiting Lab → Lab Clean (finished) or Lab Dirty → Terp Stripped (produces terpenes + retail distillate).
  • Cost allocation: Biomass cost comes from purchase $/lb. Operational costs (solvent, personnel, overhead) are allocated by date-range overlap with runs. Three split methods available for THCA vs HTE.
  • Analytics feedback loop: Dashboard KPIs, Supplier Performance, Strain Performance, and Department Views all draw from run yield and cost data to inform future sourcing decisions.
